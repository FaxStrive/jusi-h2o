## Article 1: Tampa Bay Water Sources — What's In Your Tap

- **Tampa Bay Water (TBW):** wholesale supplier for Tampa, Hillsborough, Pinellas, Pasco + member cities
- **Sources:**
  - Hillsborough River, Alafia River, Tampa Bypass Canal (surface)
  - C.W. Bill Young Regional Reservoir (15.5B gallons storage)
  - Cypress Creek / C.W. Bill Young Wellfield (groundwater)
  - Apollo Beach Seawater Desal Plant (co-located TECO Big Bend)
- **Desal:** 25 MGD — largest seawater desal plant in North America
- **Surface water treatment plant:** 120 MGD
- **Groundwater permitted:** 120 MGD
- **Supply blend:** Groundwater >50%, river ~40%, desal up to ~10%. Dynamic seasonal shifts (more river water wet season; more groundwater+desal dry season)
- **TBW uses monochloramines** as secondary disinfectant. City of Tampa temporarily switches to free chlorine ~3 weeks at a time (most recent May 2025)
- **Tampa CCR 2024:** Total hardness avg **185 mg/L = 10.8 gpg**. Citywide range 140–300 ppm (8–17 gpg). Floridan groundwater hardest; desal permeate softest; river water intermediate

**Sources:**
- https://www.tampabaywater.org
- https://www.tampa.gov

## Article 2: Plant City & Rural Hillsborough Well Water

- **Plant City = "Winter Strawberry Capital of the World"** — >75% U.S. midwinter strawberries. Plus citrus, nurseries, row crops
- **Plant City municipal supply:** Floridan Aquifer, 4 deep wells, 746–1,200 ft depth
- **EPA nitrate MCL: 10 mg/L (as N)** — methemoglobinemia protection. Nitrate NOT removed by conventional treatment
- **Common rural FL well contaminants:** nitrates (fertilizer/septic), pesticides, iron, H2S, coliform — especially where Floridan unconfined under sandy soils
- **Hardness:** Tampa benchmark 10.8 gpg. Plant City wells commonly 8–15+ gpg
- **FDEP Plant City CCR data** — pull actual numbers from municipal CCR before publishing specific values

## Article 3: Brackish Wells Apollo Beach & Ruskin

- **SWFWMD Most Impacted Area (MIA):** ~708 sq mi along coastal southern Hillsborough, Manatee, NW Sarasota. Confirmed saltwater intrusion into Upper Floridan. Ban on additional groundwater withdrawals (Southern Water Use Caution Area / SWUCA)
- **South Hillsborough Aquifer Recharge Project (SHARP):** Near Apollo Beach/Ruskin at US-41. Recharges up to 14 MGD via injection wells to push back saltwater front. Monitoring shows freshwater recovery
- **Brackish water definition:** TDS 1,000–10,000 mg/L
- **BWRO membranes:** Rated up to ~4,000–5,000 ppm TDS
- **SWRO membranes:** Required above 15,000–25,000 ppm
- **Typical BWRO:** 8–16 bar operating pressure; 55–70% recovery
- **Aquifer layering:**
  - Surficial Aquifer ~0–300 ft (sand/shell)
  - Intermediate Aquifer
  - Upper Floridan (deeper, confined)
- Coastal test wells show freshwater/saltwater transition 500–975 ft depending on location
- **EPA secondary standards:** Chloride 250 mg/L, TDS 500 mg/L, sodium 20 mg/L (low-sodium diet) / 60 mg/L (taste)

**Sources:**
- https://www.swfwmd.state.fl.us (SWUCA, MIA, SHARP)
- USGS WRI 92-4174, OFR 2013-1088

## Article 4: Restaurant & Brewery Water Treatment

- **Chloramine NOT efficiently removed by standard GAC** — requires catalytic carbon. Causes corrosion + off-flavors in espresso/coffee
- **Hardness damage:** Calcium carbonate scale forms in espresso boilers, group heads, ice machines, steam ovens. Scale inhibitors fail above high heat → softening resin or RO required
- **Brewing water profiles (verified):**
  - West Coast IPA: sulfate:chloride 3:1+ (high sulfate ~300+ ppm SO4, ~50 ppm Cl) for crisp bitterness
  - NEIPA/Hazy: ratio reversed (Cl > SO4; 150–200 Cl / 50–100 SO4)
  - Stout: higher Cl for malt body; bicarbonate/alkalinity tolerated
  - German lager: SO4:Cl 0.4–0.6, low overall sulfate
  - Sulfate astringency threshold ~400 ppm
- **NSF/ANSI standards:**
  - NSF 53: health contaminants on carbon (lead, cysts, VOCs, THMs)
  - NSF 58: RO systems (TDS reduction, nitrate, fluoride) — **DO NOT SAY "NSF 53 for RO"**
  - NSF 42: aesthetic contaminants on carbon
- **Commercial RO sizing:** GPD 300–4,000+ typical cafés/small restaurants. Atmospheric storage ~1× peak hour demand + booster pump 40–60 psi. Breweries 2,000–10,000+ GPD

**Sources:**
- NSF.org standards 42/53/58
- Brulosophy, BYO, Electric Brewery (brewing water)
- DuPont FilmTec manual (BWRO sizing)

## Article 5: Hard Water & Tampa Water Heaters (Tank vs Tankless)

**Tankless warranty thresholds:**
- **Rinnai:** Softening recommended >7 gpg (120 mg/L); strongly recommends/requires softening >12 gpg (200 mg/L). Warranty may be voided without softening on hard water
- **Noritz:** Hardness >12 gpg VOIDS warranty. ScaleShield, whole-house softener, or scheduled vinegar descaling
- **Navien:** Treatment above ~120 ppm (7 gpg). Cite NPE/NPN installation manual directly, not secondary blogs

**Scale accumulation (AWWA/Battelle 2009):**
- Electric storage tanks on unsoftened water: ~907 g/yr (2 lb/yr) scale
- Soft water: ~14 g/yr
- Tank heaters: 10–15 yr rated → 5–7 yr on untreated hard water
- Softeners can extend lifespan up to ~50%

**Efficiency:**
- Every 5 gpg hardness = ~4% efficiency loss and ~4% cost increase on gas storage heaters (50 gal/day)

**Descaling frequency:**
- Tankless on unsoftened Tampa water (10–11 gpg): vinegar/descaler every 6–12 months
- With softener upstream: every 2–3 years

**Anode note:** Softened water ACCELERATES anode rod consumption. Inspect every 1–2 years.

**Sources:**
- rinnai.us warranty TB-114
- noritz.com/warranty
