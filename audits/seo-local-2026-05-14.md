## Industry-Specific Local Factors

**Water Treatment / Home Services in Tampa Bay:**

1. **Florida licensing:** The site claims "Licensed and Insured in Florida." For water treatment specifically, Florida does not issue a standalone "water treatment contractor" license. If the business installs water heaters and performs plumbing-adjacent work, a Florida Certified Plumber or Registered Contractor license may be required. License number should be displayed on site (Florida law requires contractor license numbers in advertising). This is currently absent.

2. **WQA certification:** Water Quality Association certification is the industry's primary third-party credential. It should be displayed with the WQA member number, which enables entity verification against the WQA member directory. Currently shown as a text/badge claim without a member number or directory link.

3. **NSF certifications:** NSF-42, 44, 53, 55, 58, 401 are product-level certifications, not company certifications. The site should clarify this: the products/systems they install carry NSF certification, not the company itself. Framing these as company certifications could create a trust issue if a knowledgeable customer notices the distinction.

4. **Tampa Bay water hardness context:** Tampa Bay municipal water averages 135-260 mg/L hardness (moderately hard to very hard depending on source). This is a strong local content signal that the site uses well in blog content but incompletely on service pages.

5. **Review velocity risk:** The Whitespark 2026 study confirms review velocity as a critical local ranking factor, with the Sterling Sky 18-day rule identifying a ranking cliff when no new reviews arrive for 3 weeks. A business claiming 2,500+ reviews that does not actively solicit new reviews is at risk of velocity decay regardless of historical volume.

6. **Proximity factor:** Search Atlas ML study (2025) found proximity accounts for 55.2% of local pack ranking variance. The business address is in St. Petersburg (Pinellas County). For queries originating from Tampa (Hillsborough County), Sarasota, Lakeland, or Bradenton, proximity is structurally disadvantaged. This cannot be fixed but should inform the content strategy: invest heavily in city-specific landing pages with unique content to capture non-proximity organic rankings as a complementary channel.
